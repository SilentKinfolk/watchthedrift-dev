how does this work?

A Casio F-91W is a quartz watch: accurate, but it slowly drifts away from real time, and you rarely notice until it’s well off. This tells you exactly how far off it is, right now.

Point your phone’s camera at the watch and tap measure. The time on the face is read on your device — no photo ever leaves it — and compared against an internet time reference (NTP-style, corrected for network round-trip) at the instant of capture. If the reading isn’t clear, it asks you to retake rather than guess.

Because the watch shows only whole seconds, the answer is to the nearest second — exactly what you need. Nothing is stored or logged: every measurement stands on its own.
